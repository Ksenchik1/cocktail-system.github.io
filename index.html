MOJITO. Refreshing sweet rum-based long with lots of mint and lime. Traditionally, sparkling water is added to the cocktail, but you can replace it with a sprite if you prefer a sweeter taste.

BLUE LAGOON. Sweet refreshing long on vodka with the addition of blue curacao liqueur, which gives the cocktail a delicate blue hue. There is a legend that the "Blue Lagoon" was invented by the artist Paul Gauguin in Tahiti, but it is unlikely that he would suggest adding sprite to the cocktail.

COSMOPOLITAN. Sweet and sour cocktail based on citrus vodka with the addition of triple-sec and cranberry juice. Everyone who has ever seen "Sex and the City" knows about it. In the series, it is mostly drunk by women, but in fact this cocktail is loved by people all over the world, regardless of gender.

CUBA LIBRE. Refreshing low-alcohol long based on golden rum with cola and lime juice. Cola gives the cocktail an unobtrusive sweetness, and the sourness of lime makes the taste more interesting. Ideal for quenching thirst on a hot evening.

GIN TONIC. An immortal mix of gin and tonic, which is served, probably, in every bar on Earth. Herbal and slightly bitter cocktail perfectly refreshes and tones. You can decorate it with lime or cucumber, depending on whether you prefer the predominance of freshness or sourness.

MARGARITA. Salty sour cocktail on tequila with lime juice. Bartenders all over the world are very fond of creating twists for this classic cocktail, but the rim of salt almost always remains the same decoration of the Margarita.

The tradition of preparing alcoholic cocktails appeared in the middle of the XIX century. The aristocrats mixed strong alcohol with bitters, adding a little water and sugar to the glass. The word "cocktail" was first used by Jerry Thomas, who in 1862 published the book "How to mix drinks". This work became the very first directory of bartenders around the world.

Where the term "cocktail" came from in English remains a mystery today. There are various legends that associate this word with the original way of mixing drinks with a cock's tail, then brewing beer with spices, fruits and a boiled rooster.

The most plausible version is that this word appeared in honor of the cut tails of horses. This was how half-breeds were marked so that they could be more easily distinguished from thoroughbred horses. The term first passed from horses to people who, not having a proper origin, tried to get into the upper world. Then the cocktail began to be called diluted alcohol, because it also lost the purity of its origin.
